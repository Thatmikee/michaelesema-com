01 The Story

From Nigeria to Manchester

Michael Esema was born into the Esema Royal Family of Nsit Ubium, Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria, to the Late Obong (Barr.) Essien Joseph Esema, a barrister, politician, and Senior Special Assistant to the Governor of Akwa Ibom State. He grew up in Abuja, where governance and public accountability were not dinner table conversations. They were the family trade.

He studied Accounting at Benson Idahosa University in Benin City, where he was elected President of NUASA, the student body's accounting union. He ran it the way he thought institutions should be run: every naira tracked, every process documented, every gap closed. During his tenure, he hosted the President of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nigeria (ICAN) on campus and led the student push for BIU to meet ICAN accreditation standards and secure a Mutual Cooperation Agreement (MCATI). The MOU was signed shortly after, giving BIU accounting graduates 10 ICAN subject exemptions instead of the standard 7. He graduated in 2018.

His first role was at the National Emergency Management Agency (NEMA) in Abuja, working in the Director of Finance and Administration's office. It was supposed to be a starting point. It became an education. He watched how institutional leakage, small and constant, could hollow out an organisation from the inside without anyone raising an alarm.

From NEMA, Michael moved to B's Hive Hotel and Suites in Abuja as Head Accountant, managing departmental budgets of up to ₦10 million a year. He earned his Masters in Business Administration (MBA) from the Prestigious Nigerian Defence Academy at the same time. While balancing both, he overhauled the hotel's accounting systems, replacing manual processes with structures that actually held up under scrutiny. He was promoted to Assistant Manager, and that is when things got interesting.

As Assistant Manager, Michael didn't just keep the operation running. He modernised it. He identified an underused lounge space and partnered with Silent Party 9ja to launch a silent lounge experience, a format that was new to Abuja's hospitality scene. The events exceeded revenue projections by 45%. A space that was costing the hotel money started making it. That instinct, seeing a dead asset and turning it into a revenue line, would show up again later.

Outside the hotel, Michael was already building. He launched M'S Shoes, a retail venture that grew from local sales into international orders reaching Gambia and Ghana. He learned what it means to manage inventory across borders, deal with supply chain friction, and sell to markets where trust is built face to face, not through a website. It was small-scale commerce, but the fundamentals were real.

At every role, there was a version of the same problem. Not always theft outright. Sometimes stock that would not reconcile, cash that did not balance, or controls that existed on paper and nowhere else. His response was the same each time: find the gap, understand it, close it. He did that at NUASA, at NEMA, at B's Hive. What he could not fix within his scope, he documented. Over enough years, the pattern became a brief. The commercial tools meant to prevent stock loss were designed for retailers with full security teams and six-figure loss prevention budgets. Nothing existed that worked for the independent shop on a side street.

In 2023, Michael Esema moved to Manchester. He enrolled in an MSc in International Business Management at Manchester Metropolitan University and graduated with Merit in 2024. Manchester showed him a version of the same problem he had seen his whole career, only sharper.

Over 530,000 shoplifting offences were recorded in England and Wales in the year to March 2025. A 20% rise. The highest since records began. The British Retail Consortium puts the cost at over 2.2 billion pounds a year. Convenience stores alone logged 6.2 million theft incidents in twelve months. Over 2,000 incidents of violence against shop workers happen every day. And nearly half of all thefts go unreported because retailers have stopped believing anyone will come.

The chains have entire loss prevention departments. The independent jeweller on the high street has a bell on the door.

Michael saw the gap. He had been seeing it his whole life.

He founded Mykei Securities Ltd in 2026 and designed the ADN-1 Active Forensic Defence Node from scratch. He taught himself embedded systems programming, wrote the firmware in C/C++, wired up dual Time-of-Flight sensors, built the cloud integration on AWS IoT Core, and assembled a working prototype on his kitchen table. Then he put on a jacket and walked the streets of Manchester, knocking on shop doors, sitting with owners, hearing their stories, and asking them to trust something that did not exist yet. Five of them said yes.

He filed a patent covering 17 claims. He built the company website. He wrote the business plan. He designed the pricing model. Not because he wanted to do everything himself, but because someone had to go first.

The idea behind the ADN-1 is simple. Michael calls it Economic Sterilisation. When someone sweeps a shelf clean, the device detects it and deploys synthetic DNA forensic markers onto the stolen goods. Invisible. Non-toxic. Permanent. Every tagged item is linked to the specific crime, the specific store, and the exact time it happened. The goods become unsellable. Not because they are damaged, but because they are now forensic evidence. Theft stops making financial sense.

Every role Michael Esema has held came down to the same discipline: find what is leaking, understand why, and close it. Student union finances at Benson Idahosa. Agency accounts at NEMA. Hotel stock at B's Hive. Retail merchandise on the high streets of Manchester. The industries changed. The problem did not. He did not start Mykei Securities because he discovered asset loss late in his career. He started it because he had spent years building the competence to do something about it properly.

He carries his father's name. Not by inheritance. By what he builds with it.

Michael Esema is based in Manchester, United Kingdom.

"Most people in security started in technology and found a business problem. Michael started in governance, found a broken system, and built the technology to fix it."

02 The Product

What I'm building

Patent Pending, MYK-PAT-001

ADN-1 Active Forensic Defence Node

Mykei Securities Ltd  ·  Hardware-Enabled SaaS

A shelf-mounted IoT device designed for independent jewellers, off-licences, and convenience stores. Dual VL53L0X Time-of-Flight sensors detect when multiple items are swept from shelves simultaneously, the signature pattern of organised retail crime. Upon detection, the device autonomously deploys a synthetic DNA forensic marker mist onto the stolen goods.

The forensic marker is invisible, non-toxic, and permanent. It links every tagged item to the specific crime event, store, and timestamp. Tagged goods become commercially worthless. Any attempt to resell them creates a forensic trail directly to the handler.
